# Corporate on Demand — Hype & Marketing Playbook

## Unique Selling Points

- **Not a framework — it's a running company.** MetaGPT/CrewAI give you tools. Corporate on Demand IS a functioning autonomous org with 16 departments.
- **Proof, not promises.** Live arcade platform with 7 playable retro games = tangible output people can touch.
- **Runs while you sleep.** Cron-driven autonomy. No human in the loop.
- **Anti-slop governance.** QA department that rejects garbage. Directly addresses the #1 criticism of AI-generated code.
- **Corporate metaphor is instantly understandable.** Everyone knows what a CEO, CTO, DevOps team does.

---

## Phase 1: Launch Week (Days 1-7)

### Platform Priority Order

**1. Twitter/X (Day 1 — THE launch platform)**

AI Twitter is the epicenter. AutoGPT, BabyAGI, MetaGPT ALL went viral on Twitter first.

Thread format (threads outperform single tweets 5-10x):

Hook tweet:
> "I built an AI company with 16 departments — CEO, CTO, R&D, QA, DevOps — and let it run overnight.
>
> It built 7 retro arcade games while I slept.
>
> No human in the loop. No supervision. Just cron jobs.
>
> Here's what happened 🧵"

Follow with: screenshot of department structure → game screenshots → architecture diagram → link to live demo → link to GitHub → "star if you want to see where this goes"

Post **Tue-Thu, 9-11 AM EST**.

**2. Reddit (Day 1-2)**

- **r/LocalLLaMA** — Most important. Post as "I built X, here's what I learned" not promotion.
- **r/artificial** — General AI audience
- **r/singularity** — They love "AI doing things autonomously"
- **r/SideProject** — The arcade angle plays well here
- **r/programming** — Frame it as an architecture post

Title format: "I let 16 AI agents run as a company overnight. They built 7 arcade games."

**3. Hacker News (Day 2-3)**

"Show HN: Corporate on Demand – 16 AI agents running as departments, building software autonomously"

Post **Tue-Thu, 9 AM-12 PM Pacific**. Lead with technical architecture. Mention anti-slop governance — HN will love that.

**4. YouTube (Day 1, coordinated with Twitter)**

- Short demo video (60-90 sec): screen recording, departments activating, game being generated, playable result
- Longer walkthrough (5-10 min): architecture, department communication, anti-slop pipeline
- YouTube Shorts / TikTok cut for virality

**5. dev.to / Hashnode (Day 3-4)**

Technical deep-dive: "How I Architected a 16-Department AI Company That Builds Software Autonomously"
Cross-post to Medium for SEO.

**6. Product Hunt (Day 5-7)**

Launch Tuesday or Wednesday. Tagline: "An AI company that builds software while you sleep"

## Phase 2: Content Strategy (Weeks 2-4)

### Content ranked by engagement:

1. **Live demos / videos** — AutoGPT's demos drove 90% of its virality. SHOW the games. SHOW the terminal. SHOW the cron jobs firing.
2. **"I slept and woke up to..." threads** — The overnight narrative is gold.
3. **Architecture diagrams** — The 16-department org chart is inherently shareable.
4. **Failure/learning posts** — "My AI CTO fired the wrong department" type stories.
5. **Comparison posts** — "Corporate on Demand vs MetaGPT vs CrewAI" positioning.

### Recurring Calendar:

- **Monday:** "Weekend autonomy report" — what the system built
- **Wednesday:** Technical deep-dive on one department
- **Friday:** Fun/weird things the AI departments did

---

## Phase 3: Community Building (Weeks 2-8)

1. **Discord server** — Channels per department (#ceo-logs, #qa-reports) streaming real activity. People will watch this like a fish tank.
2. **"Adopt a department" contributor model** — Let people own/improve specific departments.
3. **Good first issues** — Label 10-15 issues. Hacktoberfest-ready.
4. **README that sells** — Hero image (org chart), one-liner, GIF of system running, Quick Start in <5 commands.
5. **Live streams** — Stream the system running. "Watch AI agents build software in real-time."

## What Worked for Viral Projects

| Project | Key Viral Mechanic |
|---|---|
| AutoGPT | "AI that does tasks by itself" — Twitter threads with demos |
| BabyAGI | Simplicity (140 lines), easy to fork |
| MetaGPT | Academic paper + "software company simulation" framing |
| CrewAI | Role-based agents = intuitive mental model, great docs |
